American Beauty

30/Jan 2016
film

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

I have probably seen this film six or seven times at this point, and it remains one of my favorite movies of all time.

“American Beauty” is the story of Lester Burnham (Kevin Spacey), a middle-aged father who awakens from his mid-life slump thanks to his infatuation with his daughter’s best friend.

Some of you are already turned off by the description and ready to skip this film; I urge you to reconsider. While the premise of the film is based on a Lolita relationship, the film is more focused on what it has to say about youth, happiness, power, beauty, and life itself.

The cinematography and the performances by the cast, coupled with Thomas Newman’s beautiful score, are reason enough to see this film. Kevin Spacey’s performance is a marvel and a treat to watch; he plays a character where we see all of his worst qualities, and yet we still love him and root for him all the same.

It’s a complex and different kind of film than you are probably used to, one where there are no truly good or bad characters and where there are moments so funny that it hurts to keep laughing and others that are so tragic that we are surprised to find ourselves so deeply moved. Almost everyone can relate to the problems of growing old, finding love and happiness, and losing relevancy in their lives, and that’s what makes “American Beauty” such a powerful and great film.

Director: Sam Mendes

Starring: Kevin Spacey, Annette Bening

R, 122 min
