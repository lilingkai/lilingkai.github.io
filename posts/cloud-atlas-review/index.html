Cloud Atlas

31/Jan 2016
film

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

A daring work of art showcasing what the magic of cinema has to offer! “Cloud Atlas” manages to be a science-fiction, historical drama, action, mystery, thriller, romance, comedy, and much more. There are six different story lines, spanning from the 1800’s to the 2300’s or so, all told together beyond the bounds of narrative continuity. The same actors are used in multiple story lines, disguised in makeup and costumed so cleverly that sometimes they are unrecognizable.

The film is almost 3 hours, yet I was never bored. To be honest, I wouldn’t have minded if it were longer just to flesh out some of the story lines. I normally would attempt to give a short description of the plot, but I think I would just lose you all the same in trying to describe the six different timelines and all their characters. Sometimes, it is better to just sit back and watch.

Yet, such a complex film warrants discussion as to what it all means. I can throw out a few ideas: connection, separation, time, friendship, love, life. But to pinpoint the film to a single ideology is perhaps as fruitless as trying to catch the clouds. I like to think of “Cloud Atlas” as a film to be enjoyed much like how one sits back and watches the clouds naturally transform to different shapes, taking our imaginations on a grand adventure.

Director: Tom Tykwer, Andy and Lana Wachowski

Starring: Tom Hanks, Halle Berry, Jim Broadbent, Hugo Weaving, Jim Sturgess, Doona Bae, Bem Wjosjaw, Keith David, James D’Arcy, Xun Zhou, David Gyasi, Susan Sarandon, Hugh Grant

R, 172 min.
