Moonrise Kingdom

11/Jan 2016
film

★ ★ ★ ★

I feel and see the nostalgia of summer’s end, first love, and youth itself in “Moonrise Kingdom”. Crisp visuals and endearing performances drive this film, and while the ending might be a bit harried, I still smile at the thought of this wonderful little fictional island. It’s easy to let yourself become spellbound by the mini-universes that Wes Anderson creates in his films, and this one is no different.

The film is set during the summer of 1965 on a New England island where our hero, Sam (Jared Gilman), attends Khaki Scout Camp, and our heroine, Suzy (Kara Hayward), lives with family. Sam and Suzy met last summer and have been plotting their escape from their current living situations since then. The island isn’t very big, but they’re confident they can find their own hideout.

Their disappearance sparks a search-and-rescue mission from the adult world, led by the island’s only police officer (Bruce Willis), the scoutmaster (Edward Norton), and Suzy’s parents, (Bill Murray and Frances McDormand). Even though the world of adults and the world of children were almost always shown separately, I had the feeling that they were not so different from each other in that both were driven by desires and emotion, and that they were all people just trying to figure out life for themselves.

Or perhaps I’m getting ahead of myself. After all, the film has its own on-screen narrator, plays out and looks like a children’s storybook, and has a character who is only referred to as Social Services (Tilda Swinton). Life as it is portrayed in the film isn’t quite like ours, but you might be surprised to find a little bit of yourself in “Moonrise Kingdom”.

Director: Wes Anderson

Starring: Jared Gilman, Kara Hayward, Bruce Willis, Edward Norton, Bill Murray, Frances McDormand, Tilda Swinton

2012, PG-13, 96 min
